# Good Faith Violation Guardrails

## The Constraint
In a cash account, buying a security with unsettled funds and then selling it before those funds settle triggers a Good Faith Violation (GFV). Three GFVs result in a 90-day account restriction.

## Professional Implementation
- **Lot Tracking**: The system must track not just total settled cash, but the specific settlement status of individual asset lots.
- **Sell-Side Lockout**: If the Portfolio Manager Agent proposes a sell order, a custom tool verifies if the shares were purchased with settled funds. If they were purchased with unsettled funds, the tool hard-blocks the sell order until T+1 midnight.

## Evolutionary Mechanism
If a GFV warning is received via the Alpaca API, the Meta-Review Crew logs a critical "Constraint Breach." It dynamically increases the safety buffer in the ledger calculation, evolving the system to wait T+2 for safety if the broker's clearinghouse is consistently slow.

## 🛑 VC Challenge & Investor Critique
**The Market Risk Paradox:** A VC will immediately spot the fatal flaw in a hard sell-side lockout. If the system buys a stock with unsettled funds (perfectly legal) and the stock suddenly drops 50% on bad news, the "Guardrail" prevents the agent from cutting its losses to avoid a GFV. A $100 account cannot survive a 50% drawdown just to satisfy an arbitrary compliance script. Sometimes, taking the GFV strike is the mathematically correct decision.

### Critical User Journeys (CUJs)
1. **The Doomsday Hold:** Agent buys $20 of XYZ. XYZ announces bankruptcy an hour later, dropping 80%. The agent tries to fire a stop-loss. The `Sell-Side Lockout` blocks it to prevent a GFV. *Result: Portfolio loses 16% of total capital to avoid a regulatory warning.*
2. **The Phantom Warning:** Alpaca API sends a generic GFV warning (strike 1 of 3). The CrewAI system panics and halts all trading for 90 days.

## 🛠️ Refinement & Architecture Update
To make this robust:
1. **Dynamic Risk vs. Compliance Routing**: The `ExecutionGuardrailService` must not be a dumb hard-block. It must calculate the EV of the block. If the proposed stop-loss execution prevents a catastrophic loss (>10% of total portfolio equity), the system is explicitly permitted to *intentionally trigger a GFV* (up to a maximum of 2 strikes per 12 months) to preserve capital.
2. **Stateful Strike Tracking**: The external PostgreSQL database must maintain a `gfv_strike_count` table. The Flow allows the intentional GFV *only* if `strike_count < 2`. If `strike_count == 2`, the hard-block becomes absolute to prevent the 90-day liquidation lock.
