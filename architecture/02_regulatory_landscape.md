# Regulatory Landscape and Capital Constraints

## The $100 Initial Capital Constraint
The defining and most restrictive parameter of this trading system is the initial capital constraint of $100. Architecting an institutional-grade AI system for a micro-capital portfolio fundamentally alters trade execution structures, necessitating strict adherence to domestic financial regulations and settlement mechanics.

## Evolution of the Pattern Day Trader (PDT) Rule
Historically, retail participants operating margin accounts were governed by the FINRA Pattern Day Trader (PDT) rule, which mandated a rigid $25,000 minimum equity balance for margin accounts executing four or more day trades within a rolling five-business-day window.

## Modernization: FINRA Rule 4210 Amendments
Effective June 4, 2026, the SEC approved amendments to FINRA Rule 4210, eliminating the outdated PDT designation and the flat $25,000 equity requirement. In its place, broker-dealers implement a proportional intraday margin framework that calculates risk exposure in real-time.

Despite these modernization efforts, the new framework still imposes a baseline margin account minimum of $2,000. Therefore, an autonomous trading agent seeded with only $100 remains legally excluded from utilizing a margin account and must operate strictly within a **cash account environment**.
