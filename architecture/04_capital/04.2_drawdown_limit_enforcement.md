# Drawdown Limit Enforcement

## Surviving the Tail Risk
A $100 account is uniquely vulnerable. A single 10% loss ($10) requires an 11% gain just to break even. Strict drawdown limits are non-negotiable.

## Professional Implementation
- **The Kill Switch**: A global `Max_Daily_Drawdown` variable is set (e.g., 5%).
- **Continuous Polling**: An asynchronous script polls the Alpaca account equity every minute. If equity drops below $95.00 intraday, it terminates all active CrewAI processes, cancels all open orders, and liquidates all positions via market orders.

## Evolutionary Mechanism
The Meta-Review crew reviews the week's volatility. If the system was stopped out by the Kill Switch three times but the market rallied immediately after every time, the crew autonomously updates the `Max_Daily_Drawdown` threshold to 7%, evolving its risk tolerance to match the current market noise floor.

## 🛑 VC Challenge & Investor Critique
**The Flash Crash Liquidation Trap:** A VC will note that executing market orders during a sudden drawdown is the easiest way to bankrupt an account. If a flash crash drops your equity to $94.00, your script fires market sell orders for all assets into a momentarily illiquid order book, filling at catastrophic prices and realizing a 30% loss instead of a 5% loss.

### Critical User Journeys (CUJs)
1. **The Spread Widening Event:** Market opens, spreads are massive. The mark-to-market equity briefly dips to $94. The Kill Switch fires market orders. *Result: Catastrophic slippage on exit.*
2. **The API Throttling Panic:** The asynchronous script polls too fast during a volatile move, gets rate-limited by Alpaca (HTTP 429), and fails to trigger the liquidation when the account hits $90. *Result: The account blows past the stop-loss.*

## 🛠️ Refinement & Architecture Update
To make this robust:
1. **Smart Liquidation Execution**: The Kill Switch must never use raw market orders. The architecture must incorporate a `LiquidationFlow`. When triggered, it utilizes limit orders pegged to the current Bid + a defined slippage tolerance. If un-filled after 10 seconds, it walks the limit down. This prevents algorithmic suicide during flash crashes.
2. **Push over Pull**: Instead of inefficiently polling the API every minute (risking rate limits), the architecture must establish a persistent WebSocket connection to Alpaca's `trade_updates` stream. The state is updated reactively, triggering the `LiquidationFlow` instantly and safely when the threshold is breached.
