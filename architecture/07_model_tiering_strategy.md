# Model Tiering and Computational Cost Efficiency

## The Threat of Token Inflation
An inherent risk of deploying an autonomous system that continuously scans the market every minute of the day is rapid computational cost inflation. Querying state-of-the-art Large Language Models (LLMs) incessantly can quickly outpace the profitability of a micro-capital $100 portfolio. 

## Strategic Model Tiering
To optimize operational economics while maintaining high intelligence, the architecture will employ strategic model tiering, leveraging LangGraph's inherently model-agnostic nature.

### Efficient Tier (Fast & Low-Cost)
Models such as **GPT-4o-mini** or **Claude 3.5 Haiku** will power:
- Initial market triage operations
- Continuous data scraping and parsing
- Broad market monitoring
- Fundamental routing tasks

### Premium Tier (High Cognitive Function)
Heavy cognitive functions will exclusively route to premium models such as **Claude 3.5 Sonnet** or **GPT-4o**, handling:
- Deep dialectical reasoning (The Debate Workflow)
- Final adjudication of trade parameters (The Judge)
- Post-trade linguistic self-reflection (The Reflexion Loop)

## Economic Impact
Empirical data demonstrates that integrating multiple models based on task complexity can reduce overall operational costs by 40% to 60% compared to running a monolithic premium model, effectively preserving the micro-capital system's compounding trajectory.
