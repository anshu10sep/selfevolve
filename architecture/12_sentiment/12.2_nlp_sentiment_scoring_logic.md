# NLP Sentiment Scoring Logic

## Quantifying Narrative
To be useful in an algorithmic model, qualitative text must be converted into a strict quantitative format.

## Professional Implementation
- **Execution Task**: `ScoreSentimentTask`
- **Output Constraint**: The Sentiment Agent must output a strict JSON array assigning a numerical score between -1.0 (extremely bearish) and +1.0 (extremely bullish) for every ingested article, alongside a calculated `confidence_score` based on the article's source credibility.

## Evolutionary Mechanism
The agent tracks its own sentiment scoring against subsequent price action. If it repeatedly scores Elon Musk's tweets as +0.8 (bullish), but the actual stock price drops immediately after, the agent updates its internal sentiment weighting matrix. It learns to classify specific influencer narratives as counter-indicators (bullish text = bearish reaction).

---

## 🛑 VC Challenge & Investor Critique
**The Contextual Hallucination:** Sarcasm, double-negatives, and financial jargon routinely break basic LLM sentiment scoring. If an article says "The CEO denied rumors that the company will *not* avoid bankruptcy," the LLM might see "bankruptcy" and "denied" and output a -1.0, completely missing the double-negative bullish implication. Furthermore, "bullish text = bearish reaction" (buy the rumor, sell the news) is a market microstructure dynamic, not a linguistic one; asking an LLM to evolve its linguistic weights based on post-event price action conflates two entirely different domains.

### Critical User Journeys (CUJs)
1. **The Sarcasm Failure:** A highly influential short-seller tweets: "Wow, great job printing another billion shares to dilute your stockholders. Brilliant management!" The LLM sees "great job" and "Brilliant" and scores it +0.9. *Result: System buys into a massive short report.*
2. **The "Sell the News" Paradox:** A company announces blowout earnings (Sentiment +1.0). The price drops 10% because it was priced in. The LLM evolves to think "Blowout Earnings" = Bearish. The next company announces blowout earnings from a depressed base, and the LLM shorts it, missing a 50% rally.

## 🛠️ Refinement & Architecture Update
To make this robust:
1. **Multi-Shot RAG Prompting**: The `ScoreSentimentTask` cannot be a zero-shot prompt. We inject 5 "Golden Examples" (including sarcasm and double-negatives) directly into the CrewAI system prompt to enforce strict financial-linguistic parsing.
2. **Decouple Sentiment from Price Action**: The Sentiment Agent must *never* evolve its scoring based on price action. Its *only* job is to accurately translate text to a vector (-1 to 1). The relationship between that text vector and the price action (the "Sell the News" effect) is handled exclusively by the `Macro Agent` and the `Portfolio Manager Flow`. If good news causes a drop, the Manager Agent learns the structural dynamic, while the Sentiment Agent's definition of "Good News" remains linguistically pure.
