# Systemic Risk Evaluation Prompts

## Top-Down Analysis
The Macro Analyst acts as the ultimate veto. If the macro environment is toxic, individual setups don't matter.

## Professional Implementation
- **Prompt Engineering**: "You are a global macro strategist. You do not care about individual stocks. Your only job is to evaluate if the systemic liquidity environment supports long-biased or short-biased trading today."
- **Output Format**: Must output a `SystemicRiskLevel` (Low, Medium, High, Extreme).

## Evolutionary Mechanism
If the Macro Agent repeatedly flags "Medium" risk during a period of sustained market selling, leading the Portfolio Manager to take bad trades, the `Meta-Review Crew` steps in. It rewrites the Macro Agent's prompt to force a heavier reliance on bond yield curve inversions and credit spread widening, forcing it to be more sensitive to systemic stress in future evaluations.

---

## 🛑 VC Challenge & Investor Critique
**The Prompt Bloat Paralysis:** "Forcing a heavier reliance" on bond yields via prompt engineering means appending paragraphs to the system prompt. Over 6 months of evolution, the Macro Agent's prompt will grow from 500 words to 5,000 words of contradictory rules ("Pay attention to bond yields", "But ignore yields if CPI is low", "Unless jobless claims are high"). The LLM's attention mechanism will collapse under the weight of its own evolved rules, resulting in entirely random `SystemicRiskLevel` outputs.

### Critical User Journeys (CUJs)
1. **The Contradictory Evolution:** The Meta-Review Crew adds a rule in January to "Heavily weigh oil prices." In July, it adds a rule to "Ignore commodity noise." In September, oil spikes. The agent's prompt contains both rules. It hallucinates a response, outputting "Low Risk" while the market crashes.
2. **The "Extreme" Default:** After taking a few losses, the Meta-Review Crew rewrites the prompt to be hyper-sensitive to any risk. The Macro Agent outputs "Extreme Risk" every single day for 3 months, preventing the $100 account from taking a single trade.

## 🛠️ Refinement & Architecture Update
To make this robust:
1. **Vectorized Rules Engine (RAG over Prompting)**: We cap the core system prompt at 500 words permanently. Evolved rules (e.g., "Yield curve inversions are critical") are stored as distinct embeddings in the Mem0 vector database. The Macro Agent uses a `Query_Historical_Rules` tool to pull only the top 3 most relevant rules for the *current* situation, preventing context window bloat and contradiction.
2. **Objective Override Thresholds**: The `SystemicRiskLevel` cannot be purely subjective. The Python execution wrapper injects a hard override: `if VIX > 30: SystemicRiskLevel = 'Extreme'`. If the LLM tries to output 'Low' while VIX is 35, the Flow overrides the LLM, ensuring objective reality always trumps semantic hallucination.
