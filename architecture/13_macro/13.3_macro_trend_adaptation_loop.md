# Macro Trend Adaptation Loop

## Shifting Strategies Based on Regimes
A strategy that works in a zero-interest-rate environment (ZIRP) fails in a quantitative tightening (QT) environment.

## Professional Implementation
- **Regime Classification**: The Macro Agent explicitly classifies the current market regime (e.g., "Inflationary Growth", "Deflationary Contraction").
- **Strategy Selection**: The Manager Agent loads different trading tools and sub-crews depending on the active regime classification.

## Evolutionary Mechanism
The Macro Agent continuously tests its own regime classification against sector performance. If it classified the regime as "Deflationary" but observes that cyclical tech stocks are vastly outperforming defensive utilities, it triggers a self-correction. It reclassifies the regime to "Growth" and updates its internal classification weighting matrix to prioritize price action over lagging economic indicators, evolving its macro thesis in real-time.

## 🛑 VC Challenge & Investor Critique
**The Regime Whiplash Effect:** Macro regimes do not change overnight; they transition over quarters. If you allow an autonomous agent to reclassify the global macroeconomic regime from "Deflationary" to "Growth" based on a few weeks of tech stock outperformance, the system will suffer from violent regime whiplash. It will constantly swap its entire toolset and strategy at exactly the wrong moments, getting chopped up in transition zones.

### Critical User Journeys (CUJs)
1. **The Bear Market Rally Trap:** We are in a structural "Deflationary Contraction." A 3-week bear market rally causes tech stocks to surge. The Macro Agent reclassifies the regime to "Inflationary Growth" and authorizes the Manager to deploy maximum leverage into tech. The rally fails the next day. *Result: Account is wiped out buying the top of a bear market rally.*
2. **The Tool Swap Crash:** The regime changes from "Growth" to "Contraction." The Manager dynamically unloads the `Momentum_Tools` and loads the `Mean_Reversion_Tools`. However, an open trade initiated under the "Growth" regime requires a momentum tool to manage its exit. *Result: Unmanaged position due to runtime dependency removal.*

## 🛠️ Refinement & Architecture Update
To make this robust:
1. **Hysteresis Thresholding**: Regime classification cannot flip easily. We implement mathematical hysteresis. The "Growth" classification requires a confidence score of >0.8 to initiate, but will only downgrade if the score drops below <0.4. This creates a "sticky" regime identification that ignores 3-week counter-trend rallies.
2. **Regime-Agnostic Exits**: Tool loading must be isolated to *entries*. Open positions must always retain access to the tools they were opened with. The `Execution Flow` guarantees that exit management nodes are perpetually active, regardless of the overarching macro regime classification.
