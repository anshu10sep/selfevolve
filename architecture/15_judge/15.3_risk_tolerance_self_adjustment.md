# Risk Tolerance Self-Adjustment

## Dynamic Drawdown Management
A static risk profile will bankrupt a $100 micro-capital account during a volatile week.

## Professional Implementation
- **Baseline Variable**: The system maintains a global `Risk_Multiplier` (e.g., 1.0).
- **The Judge's Math**: Stop-losses and take-profits proposed by the Technical Agent are widened or tightened by the Judge using this `Risk_Multiplier`.

## Evolutionary Mechanism
This is the core of the Judge's evolution. Every Friday, the Judge reviews the account equity curve.
- If equity has dropped from $100 to $90 (a 10% drawdown), the Judge autonomously modifies the global database, changing `Risk_Multiplier` to `0.5`. 
- On Monday, all stop-losses are strictly tightened by 50%, and tranche sizes are halved. The Judge has evolved from an aggressive stance to capital-preservation mode without any human intervention.

---

## 🛑 VC Challenge & Investor Critique
**The Death by Whipsaw:** Reducing risk parameters *after* a drawdown is a classic retail trading fallacy. By the time equity drops from $100 to $90, the volatile regime that caused the loss is often ending, and a mean-reversion rally is beginning. If the system halves its position sizes on Monday, it will only capture 50% of the upside during the recovery rally, permanently impairing the $100 account. VCs call this "selling the bottom."

### Critical User Journeys (CUJs)
1. **The Recovery Miss:** System loses $10 on Thursday. Halves risk to `0.5` on Friday. Monday the market surges 5%. The system makes $2.50 instead of $5.00. It takes twice as long to recover the initial drawdown.
2. **The LLM Math Hallucination:** The Judge Agent miscalculates "tightening a stop loss by 50%". It moves a stop-loss from $100 to $50 (widening risk massively) instead of $100 to $95. *Result: Instant account liquidation on the next trade.*

## 🛠️ Refinement & Architecture Update
To make this robust:
1. **Forward-Looking Volatility Adjustments (VIX/ATR)**: The `Risk_Multiplier` must *never* be based purely on retrospective account drawdown. It must be a function of forward-looking market volatility. We utilize a Python calculation: `Risk_Multiplier = 1 / Normalized_VIX`. If volatility expands, position sizes shrink dynamically, protecting capital *before* the massive drawdown occurs.
2. **Deterministic Bracket Math**: The Judge Agent does not calculate stop-loss adjustments. The LLM outputs its thesis. The `RiskExecutionNode` calculates the exact stop-loss bracket using the target asset's current Average True Range (ATR) multiplied by the `Risk_Multiplier`, ensuring perfect, hallucination-free risk math.
