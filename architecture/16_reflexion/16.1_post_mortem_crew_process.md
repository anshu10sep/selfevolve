# Post-Mortem Crew Process

## Institutionalizing Reflection
Evolution requires dedicated compute time strictly for analyzing past actions, separated from active market scanning.

## Professional Implementation
- **The Meta-Review Crew**: A specialized Crew instantiated only after the market closes (e.g., 16:15 EST).
- **Task Pipeline**: 
  1. Retrieve all closed trades from Alpaca for the day.
  2. Retrieve the explicit `ExecutionSignal` generated by the Judge Agent for those trades.
  3. Re-run the `Debate_Log` to see which sub-agent convinced the Judge.

## Evolutionary Mechanism
This Crew acts as the engine of evolution. It labels every Sub-Agent's daily performance as "Accurate" or "Inaccurate." If the Technical Agent pushed for a buy that resulted in a stop-loss, the Meta-Review Crew generates a specific `CorrectionDirective` targeting the Technical Agent for the next session.

---

## 🛑 VC Challenge & Investor Critique
**The Hindsight Bias Trap:** VCs know that analyzing a trade *after* the market closes introduces massive hindsight bias. If a trade hits a stop-loss, the Meta-Review Crew will automatically assume the `Technical Agent` was "Inaccurate" and punish it. However, in stochastic environments, a perfectly calculated 80% probability trade still loses 20% of the time. Punishing an agent for variance leads to catastrophic over-correction and model degradation.

### Critical User Journeys (CUJs)
1. **The Variance Punishment:** The Fundamental Agent makes a brilliant, mathematically sound EV+ decision. A freak news event (CEO heart attack) crashes the stock. The Meta-Review Crew labels the agent "Inaccurate" and rewrites its logic to avoid that sector forever. *Result: The system deletes a winning strategy due to random noise.*
2. **The "Lucky" Reward:** The Sentiment Agent hallucinates a completely wrong thesis. The system buys. An accidental short-squeeze pushes the stock up 50%. The Meta-Review Crew labels the Sentiment Agent a "Genius" and heavily weights its future hallucinated outputs.

## 🛠️ Refinement & Architecture Update
To make this robust:
1. **Brier Score over Binary Outcomes**: The system must never label an agent simply "Accurate" or "Inaccurate" based on the outcome of a single trade. Agents must be graded using a **Brier Score** over a rolling 30-trade window. The score measures the accuracy of the agent's *probability estimates*, not just the binary win/loss of the final trade.
2. **Decouple Outcome from Decision Quality**: The Meta-Review Crew must be explicitly prompted to ignore the final PnL when reviewing the `Debate_Log`. It must be given a deterministic `Market_Context_Replay` tool. Its only job is to evaluate if the agent followed its strategic constraints given the data available *at that exact minute*, completely isolating decision quality from market variance.
