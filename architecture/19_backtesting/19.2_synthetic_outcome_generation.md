# Synthetic Outcome Generation

## Creating the Feedback Loop
In backtesting, trades aren't actually sent to an exchange. The system must synthesize the outcome to trigger the reflection cycle.

## Professional Implementation
- **The Execution Simulator**: When the `Judge Agent` outputs a `BUY` signal, the `AlpacaOrderSubmissionTool` (Mocked) intercepts it.
- **Outcome Calculation**: The simulator looks ahead in the historical data array to see if the proposed `stop_loss_price` or `take_profit_price` was hit first, accounting for synthetic slippage based on historical volume profiles.

## Evolutionary Mechanism
The simulator feeds the synthetic PnL back into the `Meta-Review Crew`. If the system repeatedly suffers synthetic stop-outs due to underestimating historical slippage on penny stocks, the system autonomously increases its `expected_slippage_multiplier` variable, evolving its execution safety buffers based purely on synthetic failures.

## 🛑 VC Challenge & Investor Critique
**The Liquidity Illusion:** A VC will immediately challenge "synthetic slippage based on historical volume." Simulators routinely assume that if your stop-loss was $10, and the candle traded through $10, you were filled at $10. In reality, on a volatile penny stock, you might be the 500th order in the queue, and you get filled at $8. Simulators almost always under-estimate slippage, leading to an artificially inflated Expected Value (EV) that will collapse in live trading.

### Critical User Journeys (CUJs)
1. **The Gap Down Fantasy:** A stock closes at $10. The system sets a stop at $9. Overnight, terrible news hits. The stock opens the next day at $5. The Execution Simulator simplisticly calculates the loss at $9 because "that was the stop." In reality, the $100 account just lost 50% on a market-open gap.
2. **The Slippage Optimization Trap:** The system optimizes its `expected_slippage_multiplier` perfectly for the synthetic environment. Because the synthetic environment is inherently flawed, the system essentially optimizes itself to trade a video game, making it entirely unprepared for the live Alpaca API.

## 🛠️ Refinement & Architecture Update
To make this robust:
1. **Pessimistic Fill Routing**: The Execution Simulator is rewritten to be aggressively pessimistic. 
   - If a gap occurs, the fill is ALWAYS calculated at the Open price of the gap candle, not the limit price.
   - For any trade on an asset with <$5M daily volume, the simulator automatically applies a 2% penalty to the fill price to simulate worst-case liquidity queueing.
2. **The Live-Paper Pivot**: Recognizing that synthetic execution is flawed, the architecture minimizes time spent in Phase 1 (Backtesting). Phase 1 is only used to ensure the Python code doesn't crash. The true evolutionary mechanism is delayed until Phase 2 (Alpaca Paper API), where the broker handles the synthetic fills using real-time, live order book depth, providing a 10x more accurate simulation of liquidity.
