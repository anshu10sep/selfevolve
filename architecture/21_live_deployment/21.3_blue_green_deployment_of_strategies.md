# Blue/Green Deployment of Strategies

## Safe Transitions in Production
Swapping the entire trading logic of a live system is dangerous. A bad update can execute immediately.

## Professional Implementation
- **Blue Environment**: The current live ECS container trading the $100 account.
- **Green Environment**: The new container with the updated `Meta-Review` evolved prompts.
- **Traffic Routing**: The Green environment is deployed but restricted to paper trading.

## Evolutionary Mechanism
The Orchestrator monitors the Green (Paper) environment. If Green outperforms Blue (Live) by a margin of 5% over a 3-day rolling window, the system automatically swaps the Alpaca API keys. Green becomes Live, and Blue is demoted to the paper-trading testbed, ensuring the most evolved strategy always controls the real capital.

---

## 🛑 VC Challenge & Investor Critique
**The Capital Fragmentation Flaw:** "Swapping Alpaca API keys" implies that Green and Blue are running entirely separate portfolios. A $100 account cannot be divided. If Blue holds a $50 position in AAPL, and you suddenly swap the live keys to Green, Green wakes up, looks at the portfolio, sees AAPL, and says "I didn't buy this, my strategy doesn't support this." It might immediately liquidate AAPL at a loss just to "reset" the state to match its own internal logic.

### Critical User Journeys (CUJs)
1. **The Strategy Liquidation Shock:** Blue buys TSLA based on a swing trade strategy. Green (which uses a mean-reversion strategy) is promoted to Live. Green sees the open TSLA position, doesn't understand the swing context, and market-sells it instantly, realizing a loss.
2. **The "Lucky" Promotion:** Green takes one massive, high-risk paper trade and makes 10% on day 2. It mathematically outperforms Blue (which made 2% safely). Green is promoted to Live. Green takes the same high-risk trade with real money on day 4 and loses everything.

## 🛠️ Refinement & Architecture Update
To make this robust:
1. **Position-Level Handoffs**: You cannot swap strategies blindly. If Blue is demoted, it retains "Read-Only/Exit-Only" rights to the positions *it* opened. Green is promoted to Live but is only allowed to allocate *free cash*. Green is explicitly forbidden from touching Blue's legacy open positions.
2. **Statistical Rigor over Absolute Outperformance**: Green cannot be promoted just because it made 5% more profit. The promotion algorithm must use a **Sharpe Ratio Delta**. Green is only promoted if its risk-adjusted return (Sharpe/Sortino) is statistically significantly higher than Blue over a minimum 14-day window, ensuring it is actually a *smarter* strategy, not just a *riskier* one.
